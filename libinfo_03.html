ST. LOUIS RESEARCH LIBRARIES CONSORTIUM       

 

The St. Louis Research Libraries Consortium (SLRLC) includes the libraries of the Missouri Botanical Garden, the Missouri Historical Society, and the Saint Louis Art Museum.  These three libraries collaborate on projects that provide enhanced preservation and access to the library and archival collections of all three institutions.  The parent institutions of these libraries are members of the St. Louis Zoo-Museum Tax District (ZMD), which was created to broaden the base of tax support for them, plus the St. Louis Zoo and the St. Louis Science Center.

 

The first SLRLC cooperative project is the implementation of the Innovative Interfaces, Inc. (http://iii.com)  online library system.  The Andrew W. Mellon Foundation (http://www.mellon.org/) awarded $279,000 to The Missouri Botanical Garden to purchase this system and share it with the Missouri Historical Society and the Saint Louis Art Museum. 

 

Each of the three libraries has nationally important collections with complimentary strengths - scientific, historical, and artistic.  The ability to search all three institutions’ databases simultaneously allows users to find materials that would not be found in a search of a single institution.  For example, each institution has collections on the exploration of the western United States that now can be searched in conjunction with one another. The ease of searching all three collections in an online environment will   help users in the St. Louis community and beyond become more aware of the rich collections held by these institutions.

 

The Innovative system’s powerful text and image capabilities will encourage further collaboration on special projects, including educational and recreational programming and digitization projects that will make materials available through the Internet.  To see examples of the kinds of materials that can be made available, log on to    http://ridgwaydb.mobot.org/m obot/rarebooks and http://www.virtuallymissouri.org.
