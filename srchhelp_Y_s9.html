Simple Word Search

Type the WORD(S) you want, then press [Enter] or click Submit Search

 

Search Help

Enter one or more distinctive WORDS, including names, titles, subjects or other terms relevant to your search. For example:

medieval altarpieces
maori

Tips

A simple word search will search for records containing all the terms entered, regardless of their order or proximity. Word searches are not case sensitive; upper and lower case may be used interchangeably.

Omit initial articles in all languages (e.g. The, An, Le, Ein, Los). Use distinctive words for best results; the inclusion of common words such as "the", "with", "of", etc. reduces precision and will likely yield poor results.


Advanced Search Techniques

Truncation

Words may be right-hand truncated using an asterisk. Use a single asterisk * to truncate from 1-5 characters. Use a double asterisk ** for open-ended truncation. For example:

expression* german*
parmig**

Phrase Search

Multiple words are searched together as one phrase, if enclosed in quotation marks. For example:

"renaissance sculpture"
"sepik river"

Wildcards

A single character in a keyword can be replaced with a question mark to search for variants of the replaced character. For example:

organi?ation
poll???olo

Field Limits

Specify fields to search using the field abbreviations a: (author), t: (title), s: (subject), or n: (note).

(a:louis and a:museum) and (s:gogh)
(s:kiki) and (n:interview* or s:interview*)

Boolean Operators

Use the words and or or to indicate logical relationships among multiple search terms. Narrow your search by using the phrase and not to exclude specific words or phrases. Use parentheses to group words into Boolean expressions. For example:

(blaue reiter) and not kandin*
(brucke or reiter) and (exhib** and berlin)
