ST. LOUIS RESEARCH LIBRARIES CONSORTIUM       

 

The St. Louis Research Libraries Consortium (SLRLC) includes the libraries of the Missouri Botanical Garden, the Missouri History Museum the Saint Louis Art Museum and the Goldfarb School of Nursing at Barnes-Jewish College. These four libraries collaborate on projects that provide enhanced access to their collections.

The SLRLC libraries share a single integrated library system (ILS) hosted at the Missouri Botanical Garden. The ILS software used is Millennium, developed and supported by Innovative Interfaces. This system provides the ability to search all four institutions{237} collections simultaneously or individually. The initial installation of the ILS was made possible by a generous grant from the Andrew W. Mellon Foundation (http://www.mellon.org/).

SLRLC began in 2003 with the initial partnership of the Missouri History Museum, the St. Louis Art Museum and the Missouri Botanical Garden. Goldfarb School of Nursing joined the Consortium in 2010.
